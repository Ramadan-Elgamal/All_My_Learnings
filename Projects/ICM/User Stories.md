Here is the breakdown of who will actually use the app and what their technical permissions (Roles) should be:

### 1. The Data Creators (Role: ICU Nurse / Nursing Staff)

The nursing staff is the backbone of the ICU and the primary source of real-time data. To prevent paper from being generated, they must interact with the system continuously.

- **Who they are:** Charge nurses and shift nurses in the intensive care unit.
- **What they do in the app:**
    - Create the initial digital profile upon patient admission.
    - Input hourly vital signs, fluid intake/output, and Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) scores.
    - Upload PDF lab results or radiology images as soon as they arrive from the lab.
- **System Permissions:** **Write/Edit** access for clinical metrics and uploads. They generally do _not_ need access to the advanced AI summarization or diagnostic reasoning tools, as their goal is rapid, accurate data entry.

### 2. The Data Consumers & Executors (Role: Medical Resident / Junior Doctor)

The residents are the ones executing the daily tasks, writing detailed notes, and acting as the bridge between the data and the final treatment plan.

- **Who they are:** Doctors in their residency (نيابة عناية مركزة) managing the minute-by-minute care of the patients.
- **What they do in the app:**
    - Perform the initial clinical assessment and log the patient's detailed medical history into the system.
    - Query the RAG system to instantly retrieve specific metrics (e.g., _"What was the average blood sugar during the night shift?"_) without flipping through charts.
    - Review the alerts generated by the Autonomous Monitoring Agent and escalate them if necessary.
- **System Permissions:** **Full Read/Write** access. Full access to the RAG querying system and AI-assisted diagnostic suggestions.

### 3. The Decision Makers (Role: ICU Specialist / Attending Physician)

The specialists carry the ultimate clinical responsibility. They are under extreme time pressure and need to consume complex data rapidly to make life-or-death decisions.

- **Who they are:** Senior ICU doctors and consultants.
- **What they do in the app:**
    - Open the patient dashboard during clinical rounds to view the unified data.
    - Use the LLM "Instant Summarization" button to get a rapid, reasoned overview of the patient's status over the last 24 hours.
    - Review the differential diagnoses suggested by the AI to confirm or adjust the treatment plan.
- **System Permissions:** **Full Read/Write/Approve** access. They have the authority to finalize treatment plans and discharge patients within the system.

### 4. The Facilitators (Role: System Administrator)

Every enterprise medical software needs an admin to manage the platform's health and security.

- **Who they are:** Hospital IT staff or departmental managers.
- **What they do in the app:**
    - Create and revoke accounts for doctors and nurses.
    - Manage role assignments.
    - Monitor system health, API limits (for the LLM), and secure database backups.
- **System Permissions:** **Admin** access to system settings, but strictly **No Access** to unblinded patient clinical data for privacy reasons.
